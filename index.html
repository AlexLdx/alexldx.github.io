Alexandre Lourdeaux

I am currently a postdoctoral fellow at the International Center for Mathematics of SUSTech (Shenzhen, China).

My research focuses on various aspects of the theory of linear algebraic groups over an arbitrary field. Algebraic groups play a central role in diverse areas of both pure and applied mathematics. They provide a deep and unified understanding of the symmetries and transformations that underlie many mathematical and physical phenomena. These objects can arise as the automorphism groups of algebraic structures, such as quadratic forms, central simple algebras, or Jordan and Albert algebras.

    I am interested in :
  • Geometry of algebraic groups such as pseudo-reductive and unipotent groups;
  • Galois cohomology and Cohomological invariants.

Research Interests

Within the vast field of algebraic geometry, my research focuses more specifically on various aspects of the theory of linear algebraic groups over an arbitrary field. Algebraic groups play a central role in diverse areas of both pure and applied mathematics. They provide a deep and unified understanding of the symmetries and transformations that underlie many mathematical and physical phenomena.

My work particularly investigates Galois Cohomology and the subject of Cohomological Invariants. These objects often arise as the automorphism groups of algebraic structures, such as quadratic forms, central simple algebras, or Jordan and Albert algebras.
